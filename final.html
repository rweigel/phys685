1 Parallel Plate Capacitor

A large parallel plate capacitor has a small hemispherical bulge on its grounded plate. The bulge has radius b and bulges toward the second plate. The distance between the plates is d, d≫b. The second plate is at potential Vo​. A cross–section of the geometry is shown. Assume that the capacitor plates are infinite in extent.

  1. Find the potential everywhere inside the capacitor when b=0 (no bulge; ordinary parallel plate capacitor). Indicate the coordinate axes used on a diagram.

  2. Find the potential everywhere inside the capacitor when b=0.

Answer

1. Origin is at center of bulge with z vertically upwards.

ψo​=Vo​dz​=Vo​dr​cosθ

2. Let the boundary be a volume between the plates with the sides far from the bulge and far from the plate ends. One the four sides, the potential must match the solution to 1. At z=d, the potential must be Vo​.

Thus, a candidate solution is the answer from 1. and additional terms that approach zero far from the bulge.

ψ=Vo​dr​cosθ+rB0​​P0​+r2B1​​P1​+…

On the bulge, we require ψ=0, so

ψ(b,θ)=0=Vo​db​cosθ+bB0​​P0​+b2B1​​P1​+…

For this to be satisfied, all Bl​ must be zero except for B1​=−Vo​b3/d.

ψ(r,θ)=Vo​cosθ(dr​−db3​r21​)=ψo​(1−d2b2​r/b1​)

(The latter form is to emphasize that the perturbation due to the bulge is determined by the dimensionless constant b2/d2 and the distance scale r/b, which is greater than 1. Thus, the maximum perturbation is b2/d2 at the surface of the bulge.)

Normally we would need to explicilty address the non–bulge part of the plate (θ=π/2 and r>b), but the found solution already satisifes ψ=0 on that boundary. The potential on the four sides is the solution to 1., so the side boundary condition is also satisfied because there r≫b.

This problem is electrically equivalent to a grounded sphere in a constant electric field. Probably it was created by noting that placing grounded conductors at the region of space where ψ=0 for the grounded sphere problem and a distant parallel plate at Vo​ gives a problem that looks different but must have the same solution (from uniqueness). Another version of this problem is three parallel plates with the middle having a conducting bubble).
